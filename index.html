02 — Magic Moment

The moment it clicked

"Nice, that was fast!"

— Sara Brakeman · After the Webflow integration went live

The first time a Webflow form submission landed automatically in Salesforce — no manual copying, no spreadsheet in the middle, just the record appearing exactly where it should be — that was the moment Make.com stopped being abstract and became real.

It took a few days to build. It took 87 credits to prove. And it's been running without interruption ever since. That's the kind of work that matters.

Sara's reaction — "Nice, that was fast!" — is the four-word version of a clean integration. Fast to build, invisible to use, impossible to break the team's flow without.

I started January uncertain — asking clarifying questions, making early mistakes, finding my footing. I ended March shipping integrations, leading a project, training staff, and proactively documenting my own handover before being asked.

The through-line across all three months is forward motion, even when things broke around me — sandbox lockouts, lost admin access, sync issues that took hours to debug. The direction was always forward, even when I had to back up and try again.

The biggest personal lesson I'm carrying into Q2: celebrate the small wins. In a fast-moving environment, if you don't stop to acknowledge progress, you'll always feel behind — even when you're not.
